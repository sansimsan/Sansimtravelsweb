Beijing

Modern Marvels in Beijing

Step into the heart of China in Beijing, a city where 3,000 years of history blend seamlessly with cutting-edge modernity. Beijing, China’s vibrant capital, offers travelers a captivating journey through ancient temples, bustling markets, and monumental palaces, all while boasting a dynamic cultural scene and world-class cuisine. Begin your adventure by exploring the Forbidden City, an awe-inspiring imperial palace that once housed emperors, now standing as a symbol of China’s royal heritage. Nearby, the Temple of Heaven and Summer Palace invite you to experience the serenity and beauty of traditional Chinese architecture and garden design. No trip to Beijing is complete without a visit to the Great Wall, a wonder of the world stretching majestically across mountains and valleys. Yet, Beijing is not only a city of history. Modern landmarks like the futuristic Bird’s Nest Stadium and the 798 Art District showcase the city’s vibrant artistic and architectural innovations. For food lovers, Beijing’s culinary scene is rich and varied, featuring everything from the famed Peking duck to sizzling street food delights. Whether you're wandering the winding alleys of hutongs or marveling at skyscrapers, Beijing offers a unique perspective on the ancient and the contemporary. Dive into a city that will captivate you with its stories, sights, and spirit, revealing the essence of China at every turn.

Restaurants

Flavourful Journey

TRB Hutong

TRB Hutong in Beijing offers a refined dining experience blending European cuisine with Chinese touches. Set in a historic hutong courtyard, it’s known for its elegant ambiance, exceptional service, and meticulously crafted dishes, creating a memorable fine dining experience.

Da Dong Roast Duck

Da Dong is famous for its unique approach to Peking Duck, featuring crispy skin and minimal oil, making it a top choice for travelers looking to try the iconic Beijing dish.

Country Kitchen

Located in the Rosewood Beijing, Country Kitchen delivers a rustic yet refined experience with open kitchens and traditional Beijing and northern Chinese dishes like hand-pulled noodles and wood-fired Peking duck.

Jing Yaa Tang

This Michelin-starred restaurant serves a range of classic Chinese dishes, including expertly prepared dim sum and roast duck in a stylish yet relaxed setting.
